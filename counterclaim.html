Counterclaim

Many people argue that because of all the laws passed to eliminate discrimination, that housing discrimination does not occur anymore.

Many people say that racism in the housing market is gone now because of federal laws preventing it. The Fair Housing act of 1964 and the Housing and Community development Act of 1974 made it illegal for people to discriminate against anyone of color when selling or buying houses. Making it illegal with federal legislation should end the argument once and for all about housing discrimination. The legislation made it illegal to refuse a sale to someone based on their race or ethnicity. In the 21st century in a time where there are many different races living together and so many law passed to stop segregation, housing discrimination should be a non-issue.
